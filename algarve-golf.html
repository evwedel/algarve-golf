From Villa
The second course built in Vilamoura, Pinhal weaves through dense umbrella and Atlantic pine forest in a rolling parkland setting. A handful of ponds and a stream come into play on five holes but rarely threaten above-average golfers — the real challenge is the tree-lined accuracy test throughout. The clubhouse was fully renovated at the turn of the millennium and the panoramic verandah terrace overlooking the driving range is one of the better post-round spots in Vilamoura. GPS on buggies, good halfhouse stop. The ideal legs-off-the-plane opener.
⭐ Signature Hole
Hole 8 — Par 3 · 149m
Short but trickier than it looks. Tree-lined approach, well-bunkered green. A sneaky bogey hole on an otherwise generous course.
Watch Out
Hole 4 — Par 4 · Water
The par-4 4th is tricky with water waiting for the erring shot off the tee. Only water hazard that really bites.

From Villa
Quinta do Lago South
Ria Formosa views
The crown jewel of Portuguese golf. Designed in 1974 by William Mitchell, who introduced American-standard greens and bunkers to the Algarve. Hosted the Portuguese Open eight times — including Colin Montgomerie's 1989 win where he shot 63 and finished 11 strokes clear to break the world record at the time. The front nine favours a draw through parkland; the back nine loops around the Ria Formosa lakes and favours a fade. Water on holes 3, 15 and 17. Pristine conditioning, lightning-fast greens. The hardest holes are 8, 10, 15 and 18. A course that genuinely challenges every part of your game. Book first — it sells out faster than any other course in the Golden Triangle.
⭐ Signature Hole
Hole 15 — Par 3 over water
A showstopper. Tee shot over water to a well-guarded green — the kind of hole you'll be recounting for weeks. Get the yardage right.
Also Watch
Hole 18 — Par 4 closer
Demands a precise approach to a well-guarded green. Great closing hole with clubhouse viewing terrace behind the green.

After a €9 million redesign by Ryder Cup captain Paul McGinley in 2014, the North has emerged as a genuine rival to the South — all tees, greens and bunkers completely rebuilt. More open and playable than its neighbour, but with crafty bunkers and water challenges that keep you honest. The routing is interesting and varied. Buggy bar on course. VdL Royal — clifftop views
The famous 16th
One of the most recognisable courses in all of Europe. The Royal mixes nine of Henry Cotton's original holes with nine redesigned by Rocky Roquemore in the 1990s. Rolling fairways set among umbrella pines, beautiful lakes, and wild flowers — and then the 16th. The course has hosted the Open de Portugal in 2002 and 2003. Handicap certificate required (Men max 24). Steepish climbs on the back nine — buggy recommended. The almost-island green of the 9th is another highlight before the famous finale. Spike's restaurant at the clubhouse is one of the best golf terrace setups in the Algarve.
⭐ THE Signature Hole — Bucket List
Hole 16 — Par 3 · 224 yards (back tees)
A clifftop par-3 with a full carry over a dramatic ravine to a green more than 200 yards away. The most photographed hole in Portugal. Into the wind it is one of the most daunting shots in European golf. Take a photo before you swing.
Also Watch
Hole 9 — Almost Island Green
A scenic par-3 almost entirely surrounded by water. Tee shot must land softly. Green is saucer-shaped and hard to judge. No bailout.

Vila Sol — valley setting
Pine-lined fairways
27 holes across three nines — Prime, Challenge and Prestige — naturally sculpted through a valley landscape. You pick which two nines to play depending on mood and energy. Set on 150 hectares with diverse flora and fauna, the routing feels natural and unhurried. Twice hosted the Portuguese Open. Has an excellent grassed Academy for anyone wanting a quick lesson. At Sunday afternoon prices it's excellent value and the perfect wind-down round after the Royal's intensity in the morning. Good green speed (around 10 on stimpmeter) and well-maintained fairways.
⭐ Strategy Note
Pick Prime + Challenge for the best 18
The Prime nine is the most scenic. Challenge offers the tightest holes. Prestige is the most open. Mix based on the group's mood after a long Sunday of golf.
Watch Out
Opening 3 holes — Par 3 cluster
The first three holes are tricky par-3s. Get through them cleanly and the course opens up nicely. Don't let them wreck your scorecard early.

Old Course — Grande Dame
Umbrella pine fairways · 1969
The second oldest course in the Algarve and one of the most respected layouts in all of Portugal. Frank Pennink's 1969 design through the Vilamoura umbrella pines is a pure parkland test of accuracy and ball-striking — no gimmicks, just golf. The layout rewards clean hitting from the tee and precise iron play. Several doglegs on the back nine keep you thinking. Martin Hawtree's 1996 update improved the flow without changing the character. Hosted the Portugal Open on the European Tour. One of the highest Google ratings of any course in the Algarve at 4.6★ from 564 reviews. The perfect send-off round — satisfying, classic, memorable.
⭐ Signature Hole
Hole 4 — Par 3 over water
The only water hole on the course, and it bites. The front nine's only real drama — get it right and the back nine opens up beautifully.
Also Watch
Hole 1 — Short par-4 opener
Risk and reward off the first tee. A perfect scene-setter that tells you immediately what kind of round this course will demand.
